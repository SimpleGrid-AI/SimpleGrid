Why most pallets ship 20-30% air

Packaging gets designed once - usually around a single SKU and a stock carton size - and then nobody revisits it. Five years later that same carton goes out every day, dragging hundreds of cubic inches of void with every pallet. Multiply across thousands of shipments and the air becomes the single biggest hidden line in freight spend. The fastest win in outbound logistics is not a better rate - it is fewer pallets to ship.

Orientation matters more than people think

A 12 x 10 x 8 inch box has six possible orientations on a 48 x 40 in pallet. The best orientation might fit 20 per layer; the worst might fit 12. Same box, same pallet, 67% more units just by turning the carton. This tool runs all six orientations automatically and reports the one that maximizes units while respecting the container weight cap.

Pallet vs 20ft vs 40ft vs 40 HC

A standard GMA pallet gives roughly 13 ft^3 of usable cube. A 20ft container is about 1,170 ft^3 (or 10-11 standard pallets floor-loaded). A 40ft is about 2,390 ft^3 (or 20-22 pallets). A 40ft HC adds another 12 inches of height for an extra 290 ft^3 - meaningful for light, voluminous freight. Pick the right container for the cube and weight of your run, not the one you used last time.

When to invest in better packaging

If a 10% reduction in carton dimensions yields 1+ extra unit per layer x layers on every pallet, the math on a tooling redesign usually closes in a year. For a SKU shipping 200 pallets a year at $400 LTL each, recovering 10% of pallets is $8,000 a year in freight - and that ignores warehouse storage savings and reduced carbon footprint that many customers now scorecard.

Frequently asked questions

Does this handle mixed-product loads?

No - this tool assumes one SKU at a time. For mixed loads, run each SKU separately to see units per pallet, then combine. True 3D bin-packing for mixed SKUs needs a dedicated load planner (CubeMaster, MaxLoad Pro, or SimpleGrid's order-planning module).

What about overhang on a pallet?

This tool uses the usable pallet footprint (48 x 40 in for a standard GMA pallet). Slight overhang (up to 1 in per side) is generally tolerated by carriers but increases damage risk and may be flagged on inspection. For air freight and ocean LCL, overhang typically triggers reweigh and surcharges.

How accurate are the container internal dimensions?

Within +/- 1-2 inches. Internal dimensions vary by container manufacturer, age, and condition (lining, repairs, dunnage). For tight loads, always verify with your forwarder against the actual container's CSC plate. We use industry-standard nominal internals (20ft: 233x92x90 in; 40ft: 474x92x90 in; 40ft HC: 474x92x102 in).

Should I always max stack height?

No. Stack height is limited by product crush strength (BCT - box compression test), pallet load rating, and warehouse rack constraints. Most operations cap pallet height at 60-72 inches for double-stacking in trailers, or 48-52 inches for warehouse turnover. Set max_stack_layers based on your real handling rules, not the math.

How do I improve fill rate?

Redesign the carton to the SKU (eliminate void). Flat-pack or knock down (KD) where possible. Standardize SKU dimensions to nest with pallet footprint multiples (e.g., 12x10 in or 24x20 in cartons that tile 48x40 in pallets perfectly). Combine SKUs into mixed pallets where lane volume justifies the picking cost.
