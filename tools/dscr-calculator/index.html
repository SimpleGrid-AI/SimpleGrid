DSCR Calculator.

Debt Service Coverage Ratio - the single number every commercial banker checks before approving a loan. DSCR = annual cash available for debt service / annual debt payments. Below 1.0x, you can't cover your debt from operations. Most manufacturers don't know theirs until a bank tells them it's too low.

Current cash and debt

EBITDA = Revenue - Operating Expenses (excluding interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization). Debt service = all principal + interest on term loans, equipment loans, SBA loans, mortgages, capital leases. Some lenders include LOC interest but not LOC principal.

Why DSCR is the most important number to a banker

Banks lend against cash flow, not assets. Collateral matters as a backstop - DSCR is what tells them whether the loan gets repaid from operations, the way both sides hope it does. A 1.50x DSCR means the business throws off 50% more cash than needed to service debt. A 1.25x DSCR means it just clears. Below 1.0x, the math no longer works.

Most rejected loan applications fail one of two tests: insufficient DSCR or insufficient collateral. Of the two, DSCR is the harder fix - collateral can be added, but EBITDA takes quarters to rebuild.

Improving DSCR in 90 days

  • Add-backs. A clean EBITDA includes legitimate non-recurring items (one-time legal fees, owner perks, prior-year accrual reversals). Pair with our EBITDA Calculator - bankers will accept reasonable add-backs.
  • Refinance. Stretching a 5-year loan to 7 years cuts the annual payment 20-25%, which mechanically improves DSCR by a similar percentage. Costs you total interest but buys covenant headroom.
  • Pay down high-cost debt. Credit cards, merchant cash advances, factoring lines - all expensive AND included in debt service. Pay them off first.
  • Convert short-term to long-term. If you're funding equipment with line-of-credit borrowing, term out into an equipment loan. LOC principal is sometimes excluded; term loan principal counts but is amortized.

The covenant trap

Most term loans include a DSCR covenant - usually requiring 1.20x or 1.25x measured annually or quarterly. Trip it once, and the loan can be called or repriced. Smart manufacturers watch DSCR on a trailing-12-month basis monthly, not just at year-end. By the time the year-end measurement happens, it's too late to fix.

Frequently asked questions

What DSCR do banks require?

Commercial bank minimum is typically 1.20x-1.35x. SBA 7(a) loans require 1.15x-1.25x. PE-backed companies often have DSCR covenants at 1.20x-1.30x. Below 1.0x means operating income cannot cover debt payments.

What goes into Net Operating Income for DSCR?

For most lender DSCR calculations: EBITDA is the numerator. Some lenders use Net Income + Depreciation + Interest. Always confirm definition with your specific lender - the difference can swing DSCR by 0.2x or more.

How do I improve DSCR before applying for a loan?

Three levers: 1) Increase EBITDA. 2) Restructure existing debt to lower annual payment - longer term or lower rate. 3) Pay down high-cost debt first. Adding collateral does not improve DSCR but may improve loan terms.

What is a "comfortable" DSCR?

For manufacturers: 1.50x or higher is comfortable. 1.25x is the threshold most banks accept but offers no buffer. Below 1.25x, you are one bad quarter from a covenant breach.

Does DSCR include all debt or just term loans?

Total debt service: term loan principal + interest, equipment loan payments, SBA, mortgages, capital leases. LOC interest is typically included; LOC principal usually not. Confirm with lender.
