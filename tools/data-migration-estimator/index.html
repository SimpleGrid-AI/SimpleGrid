Why data migration breaks ERP projects

Three reasons. First, the data is worse than the team thinks - dedupe, normalization, and blank fields take 3x longer than estimated. Second, the team isn't dedicated; they're trying to do migration while keeping the shop running. Third, scope creeps - everyone wants to migrate "just in case" and the project balloons. The fix is the same in every case: less data, dedicated team, cleaning before migration.

How much history should you migrate?

Almost none. Master data (customers, vendors, items, BOMs) is non-negotiable. Open transactions (orders, POs, inventory) are necessary. Closed transactions? Most companies need 1 year max for trend reporting, 0 if you can report from the old system. Every year of history doubles the migration effort and quadruples the data quality work. Leave history in a read-only copy of the old system.
