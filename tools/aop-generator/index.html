Why most manufacturers skip the AOP - and why they shouldn't

Most owners feel like "the plan is in my head" is enough. It isn't, for two reasons. First, the team can't help you hit a target they can't see. Second, you can't measure variance without a baseline - and without measured variance, you don't know which months are problems until quarter-end. A one-page AOP fixes both. The math is simple. The discipline is hard.

Use this alongside the rest of the financial suite

Cash flow forecast takes the AOP and shows you the bank balance month by month. Break-even tells you the minimum revenue floor. KPI benchmarks show whether the targets you're setting are aggressive, realistic, or sandbagged.
