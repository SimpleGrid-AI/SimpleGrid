The 5 AIAG core tools - and why all are required

IATF 16949 requires implementation of all five AIAG core tools: APQP, PPAP, FMEA, MSA, and SPC. Each tool is essentially a separate body of knowledge - and most automotive auditors will fail a supplier on any one of them. APQP plans quality into new products. PPAP proves the parts meet spec. FMEA forces risk analysis. MSA validates the measurements. SPC monitors process stability. Together, they're the difference between making a part that works once and making a part that works for 8 years in 50,000 vehicles.

How to get certified - realistic path

Step 1: ISO 9001 certified (required prerequisite). Step 2: Train your team on the 5 core tools (AIAG workshops, ~$1,500/seat). Step 3: Implement and run for 3-6 months minimum (auditors look for evidence of operation, not just documentation). Step 4: Internal audit + management review. Step 5: Engage registrar for Stage 1 (gap assessment) and Stage 2 (full audit). Total realistic timeline: 12-18 months for a company starting from scratch.
