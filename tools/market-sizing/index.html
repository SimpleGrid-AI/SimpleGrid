TAM, SAM, SOM in one paragraph

TAM is the total addressable market - everyone who could conceivably buy the product. SAM is the serviceable addressable market - the slice you can actually reach given your geography, capabilities, and capacity. SOM is the serviceable obtainable market - the share of SAM you can realistically win in your planning horizon. The number that matters for the decision is SOM, not TAM. A $500M TAM with a 1% capture is a $5M opportunity - not a $500M one.

How to pick a realistic share

1-5% is normal for a new entrant in three years. 5-15% if you have a clear advantage (lower cost, faster delivery, unique capability). Above 15% only if you're effectively the only viable supplier - sole-source qualification, deep regulatory moat, or unique technology. Anything higher and you're fooling yourself.
