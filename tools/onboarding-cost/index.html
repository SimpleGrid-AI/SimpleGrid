Why most manufacturers underprice new customers

The first PO from a new customer feels like found revenue. It isn't - the onboarding work that came before it cost real money, and that money has to be recovered from future orders. If the customer churns after order 3 and your break-even was order 12, you lost money on the relationship even though every order you delivered looked profitable on the surface. The fix is either (a) charge an onboarding/setup fee, (b) require minimum order commitment, or (c) bake the recovery into the first-order pricing.

How to make onboarding cheaper

Standardize the package. The same PPAP template, the same FAI report format, the same control plan structure for every new customer. Maintain a sample library (one of every part you've ever made, cleanly labeled, easy to grab). Build a "new customer setup" checklist with named owners and time estimates. Every new customer that fits your standard process onboards in half the time of a custom one - and onboarding cost is mostly time.
