What Section 179 actually does

Without Section 179: you buy a $200K machine, depreciate it over 7 years at ~$28.6K/year, and your tax savings are ~$7K/year (at 25% rate) for seven years. Total tax benefit: $50K, spread out.

With Section 179: you deduct the full $200K in Year 1, save $50K in taxes immediately, and your effective cost drops to $150K right now. Same total tax savings - but the Year 1 cash impact is dramatically different. For most manufacturers, that immediate liquidity is worth more than waiting seven years.

Combined with bonus depreciation

Above the Section 179 cap (or in addition to it), bonus depreciation lets you deduct a percentage of remaining basis immediately. 60% in 2024, 40% in 2025, 20% in 2026. After 2026, it goes to zero unless Congress extends it - so 2024-2025 are the last years of meaningful bonus depreciation under current law.

The combined Section 179 + bonus depreciation can give you 100% first-year write-off on equipment costing well below the Section 179 cap. On a $200K machine in 2024 at 30% tax: full $200K deduction = $60K tax savings = effective cost of $140K. The IRS subsidizes nearly a third of the purchase price.

The "place in service" trap

Section 179 doesn't apply to equipment you've bought - it applies to equipment you've placed in service. The machine has to be installed, calibrated, tested, and actually usable for its intended purpose during the tax year. A December 28 delivery with a January 6 install date moves the deduction to next year - which can be a major surprise at tax filing.

If you're year-end buying for tax purposes: confirm the install and commissioning timeline with the vendor in writing. Don't take "we'll have it running before year-end" verbally.

Frequently asked questions

What is the Section 179 limit for 2024?

$1,160,000 deduction limit. Phase-out begins at $2,890,000 in total equipment purchases.

What is bonus depreciation in 2024?

60% in 2024 (was 100% through 2022, declining 20 points per year). 40% in 2025, 20% in 2026.

What equipment qualifies for Section 179?

Most tangible business property: manufacturing equipment, machinery, computers, software, vehicles over 6,000 lbs, office furniture, certain real property improvements. Must be used 50%+ for business.

What does "placed in service" mean?

The equipment is installed, tested, and ready for use in your business - not just purchased or delivered.

Can Section 179 create a tax loss?

No. Section 179 deduction is limited to taxable business income for the year. Bonus depreciation, however, CAN create a tax loss.
