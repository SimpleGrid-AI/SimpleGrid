What FDA inspectors actually check

The Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA) Preventive Controls rule shifted FDA inspections from "look at the facility" to "look at the documentation." Inspectors want to see: a written food safety plan signed by a PCQI, monitoring records that prove the controls actually ran, corrective action records when they didn't, and verification records (calibration, product testing, environmental monitoring). The facility itself matters less than your ability to prove the system works.

Cost of a 483 vs cost of compliance

FDA Form 483 (observations) are public. Warning Letters are public and Google-indexed. Import Alerts shut down exports. Consent Decrees can force a temporary shutdown. The cumulative cost of a single bad inspection - lost customers, increased insurance, mandatory consultant remediation, legal fees - typically exceeds $250K for mid-market processors. A well-run food safety program costs $25K-$75K/year ongoing.
