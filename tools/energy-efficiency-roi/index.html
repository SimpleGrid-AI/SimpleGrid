Why energy upgrades sit in a queue forever

Every plant has a list of efficiency projects somebody pitched at some point. LEDs. New compressor. VFD on the chiller. Solar quote from two years ago. They never get funded because nobody can produce a defensible payback number in a meeting. This calculator gives you that number in 90 seconds, with rebates and tax treatment baked in.

What payback actually means

Simple payback is "years to recover your net cash outlay." Net investment is upgrade cost minus utility rebate minus first-year tax shield - not sticker price. NPV adds the time value of money (a dollar saved in year 10 is worth less than one saved today) and energy inflation (utility rates almost always go up). If NPV is positive at your discount rate, the project beats your hurdle rate.

Rebates and incentives change the math

Most US utilities run prescriptive rebate programs for LED, VFD, premium-efficiency motors, HVAC, and compressed air. Rebates routinely cover 30-50% of upgrade cost - sometimes more for custom projects. The federal IRA also stacks bonus depreciation, ITC for solar, and Section 179 expensing. Always check dsireusa.org and call your utility account rep before locking in payback math.

Frequently asked questions

Are utility rebates really available?

Yes, and they are often substantial. Most US utilities offer rebates of 30-50% of upgrade cost for LED, VFD, high-efficiency motors, HVAC, and compressed air. Check dsireusa.org for a state-by-state database and call your utility account rep - many rebates are unadvertised.

What's the right discount rate to use?

Use your weighted average cost of capital (WACC) or your hurdle rate for capex. Mid-market manufacturers typically use 8-12%. Higher than your borrowing cost, lower than your equity return target. If you'd take a loan at 7% to do the project, use 8-10%.

How realistic is 3% annual energy inflation?

Historical US industrial electricity prices have averaged 2-4% annual increases over the last 20 years, with spikes during disruptions. 3% is a conservative baseline; in 2022-2023 many regions saw 8-15% increases. Higher inflation favors faster payback.

Does this account for Section 179 / bonus depreciation?

The tax_deductible toggle approximates the first-year tax shield (cost x tax rate). For exact treatment under Section 179, bonus depreciation, or the IRA energy tax credits, consult your CPA - actual cash benefit can be larger in year 1 than this tool shows.

Does solar really pay back in 7-10 years?

In most US markets with federal ITC (30%) plus state incentives plus net metering, commercial solar payback is 4-8 years on south-facing roofs with good insolation. Without incentives or in low-electricity-cost regions, payback stretches to 10-15 years. Get a site-specific quote.
