The difference between REACH and RoHS

RoHS is product-focused and narrow: 10 specific substances, EEE only, fixed thresholds. REACH is substance-focused and broad: any substance, any product, evolving list. RoHS has clear compliance criteria; REACH has obligations that depend on your role (manufacturer, importer, distributor, downstream user) and the quantities involved. Most manufacturers of EEE need both. Most chemical companies and chemical-product manufacturers need REACH only. Most pure mechanical / article manufacturers need REACH "communication in supply chain" only.

SVHC list - why it matters and how to track it

The Candidate List of Substances of Very High Concern grows every 6 months. Once a substance is on the SVHC list, articles containing >0.1% by weight trigger notification + communication obligations - and may eventually be banned outright under Annex XIV. The list is at echa.europa.eu/candidate-list-table; subscribe to ECHA newsletter for updates. Build the list into your ERP and re-screen each release.
