Why warranty is the most under-tracked cost in manufacturing

Warranty hits as cash outflow weeks or months after the sale - by which time the original transaction is closed, the salesperson is paid, and nobody on the floor associates today's rework with last quarter's job. It shows up in COGS as "rework" or "field service" but rarely traces back to specific products or failure modes. The result: warranty cost ages quietly and is often invisible to the people who could fix the root cause.

What "cost per claim" actually includes

Parts: typically 20-30% of total claim cost. Labor (technician time, troubleshooting, repair, paperwork): 30-40%. Shipping (inbound, outbound, sometimes expedited): 10-20%. Admin (logging, customer service, RMA processing): 10-20%. Customer goodwill damage: not in the number but real - the customer who got a defective part once thinks twice before reordering. The full cost of a warranty claim is typically 4-7x the cost of the part itself.
