The 10 dimensions and why they matter

The list isn't arbitrary - it's the dimensions a buyer comparing manufacturers actually weighs: price, quality, delivery, capabilities, expertise, service, coverage, stability, innovation, brand. Skip any one and your analysis has a blind spot. The dimension where you score highest is your moat - invest there. The dimension where you score lowest is your vulnerability - either fix it or stop targeting customers who care about it.

What to do with the radar chart

  1. If you're worse than everyone on a dimension that doesn't matter to your target customer: ignore it. Don't waste resources improving things buyers don't price.
  2. If you're worse on a dimension that does matter to your target customer: fix it or pivot to a customer segment that cares about your strengths.
  3. If you're best on a dimension nobody else competes on: that's your moat - make it more obvious in your marketing.
