The 3 clauses that cost manufacturers the most

1. Limitation of liability. Without a cap, a single defective batch can expose you to the customer's full lost profits, recall costs, and customer's customer claims. Standard: cap at 12 months of purchases or insurance limits.

2. IP and tooling ownership. If you build the tooling and the contract is silent, the customer may walk with your tooling - or worse, claim ownership of your process improvements. Make it explicit.

3. Termination for convenience. 30-day termination clauses can leave you with $500K of dedicated inventory and idle capacity. 90 days is standard for custom production; longer for capital-intensive arrangements.

What about a PO with terms on the back?

That's a contract. Sometimes the back-of-PO terms are reasonable; often they're one-sided and the buyer is counting on you not reading them. Run them through the Customer Contract Risk Checklist - /tools/contract-risk-checklist/.
