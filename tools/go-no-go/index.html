Go / no-go - disciplined evaluation in 5 minutes.

Most manufacturers say yes to everything and spread too thin. This 16-question framework forces a real evaluation across Fit, Financial, Strategic, and Risk dimensions before you commit.

Saying no is a strategic skill

The opportunities you turn down create capacity for the ones worth winning.

Every "yes" to a poor-fit opportunity is a "no" to the one you'll be too busy to bid on next month. Use this tool when the gut says yes and you want to make sure the head agrees.

The four dimensions and why all four matter

Fit - can we actually do this work? A great deal you can't deliver is a worse deal than no deal. Financial - does the math work? The most common failure mode is winning unprofitable work because volume looked good. Strategic - does this advance the company? Building capability or diversifying customers can justify a marginal deal. Risk - what's the downside? A complex job with a difficult customer at a tight deadline can damage other customer relationships when things slip.

Decision rules

  • Strong Go - all four dimensions score 15+/20. Proceed.
  • Go with Caution - one dimension at 12-14. Mitigate that dimension explicitly.
  • Needs Discussion - mixed scores. Get a second opinion.
  • No-Go - any dimension below 8, OR financial below 10. The math has to work; the rest is upside.
