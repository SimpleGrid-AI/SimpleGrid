Business continuity - are you ready for the 8 things that can stop your shop?

Most mid-market manufacturers have zero BCP. Insurance covers the financial loss; BCP covers the operational recovery. You need both. A basic plan takes 8-16 hours; the cost of not having one is potentially the entire business.

For each of the 8 scenarios, answer four questions: Do you have a plan? Is it documented? Has it been tested? Do you have insurance / backup?

The 8 scenarios every BCP should cover

Facility damage - fire, flood, severe weather. Even with insurance, recovery takes 12-18 months. Equipment failure - what happens when your CNC, oven, or press dies on a Wednesday? Key person loss - one founder hit by a bus is the most underrated BCP scenario in manufacturing. Supplier failure - sole-source suppliers are bigger risk than most owners admit. Cyber attack - manufacturing is the #1 ransomware target. Utility outage - extended power loss in winter has shut down whole regions. Pandemic - we just lived through this; surprisingly few companies updated their plans. Customer loss - if your top customer is 30%+ of revenue, the BCP is "how do we survive losing them."

Cross-link this with the rest of your risk program

Run the Insurance Coverage Audit alongside this - the gaps usually correlate. Cyber insurance won't pay if you don't have basic controls; business interruption coverage won't help if you can't prove you had a recovery plan in place.
