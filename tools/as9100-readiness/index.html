What makes AS9100 harder than ISO 9001

AS9100 adds aerospace-specific requirements that simply don't exist in ISO 9001. Configuration management requires tracking exactly which revision of which drawing was used to make which serial number of which part. Counterfeit parts prevention requires authorized distributor controls, suspect part reporting, and detection capability at receiving. Special processes (welding, heat treat, NDT, plating) require Nadcap accreditation in addition to AS9100 - which is essentially a separate certification per process. Risk management is formalized beyond ISO 9001's "consider risks and opportunities." First Article Inspection requires the AS9102 forms specifically.

OASIS, Nadcap, and the aerospace certification ecosystem

AS9100 certification is registered in the OASIS database (Online Aerospace Supplier Information System) - this is how primes verify your status. Nadcap is a separate accreditation for special processes (managed by PRI) - required if you do welding, heat treat, NDT, chem proc, etc. for major primes. ITAR/EAR export controls apply if any of your products are export-controlled. CMMC is becoming required for DoD work. Plan all of these together; they share infrastructure but the audits are separate.
