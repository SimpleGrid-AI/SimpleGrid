Why manufacturing is the #1 ransomware target

Manufacturers can't afford downtime. A bakery loses one day of orders; a manufacturer with $50M revenue and a 30-day outage loses $4M of revenue plus customers permanently switched to backup suppliers. Ransomware gangs know this and price accordingly - the average ransom for mid-market manufacturers is $850K (Coveware, 2023). The combination of legacy OT systems, flat networks, weak access controls, and high pressure to pay quickly makes manufacturing the most lucrative ransomware target on earth.

The cheapest controls give the biggest risk reduction

MFA alone blocks 99.9% of automated account compromise (Microsoft). Immutable backups turn ransomware from "pay or close" into "restore in 48 hours." SPF/DKIM/DMARC prevent email spoofing that drives most initial access. All three combined cost less than $500/year for most mid-market manufacturers. Do these first.
