What FDA auditors focus on

Batch records and supporting data top the list - inspectors look for completeness, contemporaneous signature, and traceability from raw material to released finished good. Cleaning validation is second - "show us your cleaning data" closes more facilities than any single other line. Deviation handling and CAPA effectiveness round out the top three. If those three are clean, most other findings are minor. If those three are weak, even a strong facility will get a 483.

The cost of remediation vs the cost of compliance

FDA Form 483 costs typically run $50K-$250K in consultant fees and internal time. Warning Letters add legal and PR cost; they're public and indexed. Consent Decrees can force temporary or permanent shutdown. Compare this to the cost of doing GMP correctly: $50K-$150K/year for an internal QA function plus periodic outside auditing. The math is straightforward - compliance is the cheap option.
