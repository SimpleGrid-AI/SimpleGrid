IP protection audit - is your competitive advantage actually protected?

Most mid-market manufacturers have proprietary processes, designs, or trade secrets - but no formal protection. One employee departure can transfer your competitive advantage straight to a competitor. This audit scores 15 protection points across trade secrets, patents, trademarks, and design.

IP discounts your valuation

Buyers reduce multiples when competitive advantage can walk out the door.

In due diligence, the first question after financials is "what proprietary IP do you own and how is it protected?" If the answer is "we don't really know," the offer drops by 0.5x to 1.0x EBITDA.

The four protections every manufacturer should have

NDAs with every employee and contractor. Costs $0 incrementally. Without one, you have almost no legal recourse if someone walks with your process knowledge.

"Work for hire" + IP assignment language in contractor agreements. Otherwise the consultant who designed your fixture owns the design - not you.

Document control on drawings, BOMs, and process documents. Trade secret protection requires "reasonable efforts to maintain secrecy." Marking documents Confidential and restricting access is the basic evidence.

Federal trademark on your name and key product brands. Common law rights are local and weak. Federal registration gives nationwide protection and statutory damages.

When to file a patent vs keep a trade secret

Patent: when the innovation is visible in the final product (a customer or competitor can reverse-engineer it). Trade secret: when the innovation is in your process and stays inside your walls. Coca-Cola has kept its formula as a trade secret for over 100 years - patents expire in 20.
