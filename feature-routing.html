Every SKU runs its own route.

Most ERPs force every product down one fixed routing. SimpleGrid doesn't. Each SKU carries its own sequence of stages - insert a step or an outside service between any two nodes, drag to reorder, and the system routes work by the SKU's own attributes.

Routing that bends to the product, not the other way round.

A route per SKU

No shared template forced on every product. Each SKU stores its own ordered list of stages, so a simple item and a complex one never share a line they don't need.

Outside services inline

Send work to a vendor mid-route - plating, printing, sub-assembly - as a service node dropped between two stages. It tracks like any other step.

Stage per BOM line

Components don't all start at step one. Each line on the bill of materials enters the route at its own stage, so parts converge exactly where they should.
